Purchase Invoices

 

A Purchase Invoice created within the purchasing transaction workflow (based on a Goods Receipt document created from a Purchase Order) won't create any General Journal or Inventory Journal postings, but will register the fact of being invoiced by the Vendor. You can see the invoiced quantity in the Invoiced column of the respective Purchase Order.

A Purchase Invoice created outside the purchasing transaction workflow (not based on a Goods Receipt document) records your liability to the Vendor, and updates inventory balances.

You can create new Purchase Invoices as well as view, print, and save them in electronic form for sending in e-mail.

Note that the system won't allow you to create a Purchase Invoice based on a Purchase Order if there is a Goods Receipt created based on the same Purchase Order. In this case create a Purchase Invoice based on the Goods Receipt.

 

The following documents can be created based on a Purchase Invoice:

  • Purchase Return

 

A Purchase Invoice can be created based on:

  • Purchase Order
  • Goods Receipt

 

To enter a Purchase Invoice:

 

1. Open the Purchase Invoice window by choosing Create / Purchase Invoice in the Vendors workspace. Alternatively you can create a new Purchase Invoice by selecting Create in the Purchase Invoices list or in the Purchasing Documents journal (both accessible from the Purchasing workspace).

2. Change the default date, if necessary. The date is used for General Journal and Inventory Journal posting.

3. Enter the Due Date.

4. Select a Vendor. For purchase transactions in the Vendor field you can only select companies of the Vendor type.

If the multi-currency functionality is enabled after you select a Vendor its default currency will appear in the Currency field on a Purchase Invoice and the system will calculate an exchange rate. The default currency and the calculated exchange rate can be changed, if necessary. Also the system recalculates the exchange rate if you change the Purchase Invoice date. If you change the currency the system won't recalculate already entered prices in line items, so it's advised to change the currency and the Purchase Invoice date before entering line items.

5. If the warehouse locations functionality is enabled select a warehouse in the Location field.

6. Enter line items of a Purchase Invoice by selecting Items.

If the units of measure functionality is enabled when you select an Item its default purchase unit of measure (U/M) will appear in the U/M field. After entering the Quantity U/M, the system will recalculate the Base Quantity of the Item. Note, that all inventory tracking in the Inventory Journal is done in the base unit of measure.

If the VAT financial localization functionality is enabled the system will automatically calculate VAT based on VAT codes assigned to items. You can change individual VAT codes on items, if necessary. When the Purchase Invoice is posted the system also record a negative VAT liability in the General Journal.

7. Save the Purchase Invoice. If the Purchase Invoice is created based on a Goods Receipt document the system won't generate any General Journal or Inventory Journal postings. If the Purchase Invoice isn't based on a Goods Receipt document the system will post it in both the General Journal and the Inventory Journal. Detailed information about generated posting transactions is provided in the General Journal section.
