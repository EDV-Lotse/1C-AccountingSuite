Overview

Properly entering beginning balances when starting using the system ensures correct accounting, inventory, accounts receivable and accounts payable information as well as accurate reporting. You can enter three types of beginning balances on the Start Date - Account balances, A/R and A/P balances, and Inventory balances. As the result you should have a correct Balance Sheet that matches the Inventory Balance, A/R Aging, and A/P Aging reports.

The system doesn't allow entering any transactions before the Start Date. Set the Start Date in the Settings window in the List workspace. The system will record all Beginning Balances on the Start Date.

A/R and A/P beginning balances

1. Use the A/R Beginning Balances and A/P Beginning Balances records in the Accounting workspace.

2. Select the Company and enter amounts in the reporting and foreign currencies (if the Multi Currency option is enabled).

3. Fill in the Memo field with additional information (for example, with information about invoices comprising the balance).

4. When a beginning balance record is saved the system will create a Sales or Purchase Invoice with the provided information on the Start Date, and record the amount on the A/R or A/P account. The invoice will be visible in the invoices lists, available for payment by the Receipt or Payment documents, and included in the A/R Aging and A/P Aging reports.

Help on the Inventory Beginning Balances is provided in this section.

Account beginning balances

1. Use the General Journal Entry transaction to enter account beginning balances.

Include the beginning inventory account balance amount - the Inventory Beginning Balance records don't create General Journal transactions.

Don't include beginning accounts receivable and accounts payable amounts - the Customer Beginning Balance and Vendor Beginning Balance records create General Journal A/R and A/P postings.

2. Most probably your account beginning balances transaction won't be balanced because of absence of A/R and A/P balances. To allow posting an unbalanced transaction temporarily turn on the Unbalanced GL Entry Posting option in the Settings window in the Lists workspace.

3. Use the Start Date as the transaction date.

Beginning balances walkthrough

Let's go over a simple walkthrough to give you an example of entering beginning balances.

This is your balance sheet on 1/1/2011:

Assets: Cash - $400, Inventory - $450, A/R - $300. Total - $1,150.

Liabilities and Equity: A/P - $500, Shareholder Equity - $650. Total - $1,150.

These are the steps required to enter the balances in the example (all the steps are performed in the Accounting workspace):

1. Set the beginning balances date to 1/1/2011 in the Settings.

2. Create a record(-s) with the total Value of $450 in the Inventory Beginning Balances.

3. Create a record(-s) with the Amount of $300 in the A/R Beginning Balances.

4. Create a record(-s) with the Amount of $500 in the A/P Beginning Balances.

5. Turn on the Unbalanced GL Entry Posting option in the Settings.

6. Create and post the following General Journal Entry on 1/1/2011:

Debit Cash - $400

Debit Inventory - $450

Credit Shareholder Equity - $650

7. Turn off the Unbalanced GL Entry Posting option in the Settings.

8. Verify by running reports that the balance sheet is correct and balances, the balance sheet A/R and A/P match the A/R and A/P Aging reports, and the balance sheet Inventory matches the Inventory Balances report.
